Placement Processes

The college utilizes two processes to place students: Multiple Measures and Accuplacer Placement Tests.

If you have graduated from high school or earned your GED in the last three years, the college will utilize Multiple Measures for your initial placement. If not, the college provides opportunities to practice and prepare for the Accuplacer Placement Tests.

New Student Orientation

New Student Orientation is an essential learning experience to start your first year off right. In orientation you will learn what classes to take, how to register, and the resources available to help you succeed. Additionally, you'll receive personalized advising at orientation.

First-time college students are required to participate in orientation to help ensure their academic success. Transfer and returning students are highly encouraged to participate in orientation to learn about Pima processes, services and resources.

Advising

After you have explored our academic programs, you can reach out to an academic advisor for help selecting the courses you need to earn your degree.

Pima's Counseling Faculty lead new student orientation sessions. During the orientation, you will learn what classes to take, how to register for them, and the resources to help you accomplish your goals.

Register for Classes

How to Register

Review class schedules to find out what classes are being offered in the upcoming semester. Speak with an academic advisor if you have questions about choosing classes.

If you attend new student orientation you will receive advising and learn how to register. First-time college students are required to attend orientation before registering for classes to help ensure their success.

Students then register online through MyPima, where you can manage your student account, explore class options, see your grades, and make payments. In addition to online registration, there are other ways to register.

Student ID Card

You will need a current PCC Student Identification (ID) Card to access many of our student services, such as using student labs or selling books back to the bookstore. If you are a current student, you must have your Student ID Card validated each semester.

New students

To get a Student ID Card

  1. Take a current, government issued photo ID to a Campus location listed below.
  2. A College Employee will issue the Student ID Card.

Current students

To have your Student ID Card validated for the current semester

  1. Take your current Student ID Card to a Campus location listed below.
  2. A College Employee will provide you with a semester specific sticker that is affixed to your Student ID Card.
